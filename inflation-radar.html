What's driving prices right now

Six major signals active across India's supply chain today

🌧️
Drought in South India
Deficit rainfall in Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu has cut vegetable yields. Tomatoes, onions, and green leafy vegetables are most affected. Prices may spike 30–40% by August if rains delay.
⛽
OPEC+ production cuts
OPEC+ extended voluntary cuts of 1.66 mb/d. Brent crude is holding above $88/bbl. Indian OMCs are absorbing losses but a ₹2–5/L revision looks probable within 4 weeks.
🚢
Red Sea shipping disruptions
Cargo rerouted via Cape of Good Hope adds 10–14 days and 12–18% to freight costs. Electronics and imported goods absorb this directly — retail prices follow with a 4–6 week lag.
💱
Rupee depreciation
INR has depreciated ~4% vs USD this year. Every dollar-priced import — crude, electronics, edible oils, pharma APIs — costs proportionally more. This is a slow, persistent driver.
☕
Brazil arabica crop failure
Minas Gerais recorded its lowest rainfall in a decade. Arabica futures surged 32% over 6 months. Packaged coffee prices in India haven't caught up yet — expect hikes in 6–10 weeks.
🌴
Palm oil supply recovering
Malaysian exports are recovering after last year's labour shortage. This is easing sunflower and blended oil prices. A 5–8% reduction in cooking oil costs is expected over the next 6 weeks.
